CALABAR IS MY FAVOURITE CITY

Calabar is a historic and culturally rich city in southern Nigeria, known for its lush greenery, warm hospitality, and deep roots in tradition. Once a major trading port, the city blends colonial-era architecture with vibrant local life, colorful markets, and lively festivals. Calabar is especially famous for its annual Carnival Calabar, often called “Africa’s biggest street party,” which fills the city with music, dance, and celebration. Surrounded by rivers and rainforest, it has a calm, welcoming atmosphere that sets it apart from many larger Nigerian cities.
